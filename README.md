# Learning, Memory, and Belonging

## How Older Adults Use Conversational AI Beyond Information Seeking

This repository supports the research project **“Learning, Memory, and Belonging: How Older Adults Use Conversational AI Beyond Information Seeking.”**

The study analyzes authentic interactions between older adults and a conversational AI system in community-based lifelong learning contexts. It explores how older adults use AI not only to seek information, but also to express socio-emotional needs, recover memories, discuss local heritage, ask everyday questions, and engage with topics related to health, territory, and digital inclusion.

The project combines semantic analysis, unsupervised clustering, topic modelling, and statistical analysis to identify conversational patterns and examine how response depth varies by topic and municipality.

## Research Focus

* Conversational AI and older adults
* Lifelong learning and digital inclusion
* Memory, belonging, and local heritage
* Human-centred and responsible AI design
* Socio-emotional interaction with large language models

## Repository Contents

This repository may include methodological documentation, analytical scripts, technical reports, figures, and anonymized materials associated with the study.

Raw conversational transcripts are not publicly released because they may contain sensitive personal, emotional, territorial, or health-related information.
